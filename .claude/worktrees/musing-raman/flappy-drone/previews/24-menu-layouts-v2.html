Main Menu — Layout Concepts v2

Four layout approaches for FLAPPY DRONE main menu. All use 620x640 canvas with night scene. Fixed color palette for readability.

CHOICE Pick your preferred layout

1 / "Clean Stack" RECOMMENDED
Layout: Everything centred, top to bottom: title at ~100px, slogan below, MODE picker, DRONE picker, then a big empty zone where the drone renders on canvas. CTA near bottom. Generous spacing and clean vertical rhythm.
Pros: Clearest hierarchy, easy to scan top-to-bottom, drone has ample breathing room, most intuitive for new players.
Cons: Uses a lot of vertical space for UI — less room for cinematic sky if content grows.
2 / "Compact Header"
Layout: Title and slogan compressed into a thin strip at the very top (~50px). Mode and drone pickers sit mid-screen with more breathing room between them. The drone display area gets more vertical real estate. Maximizes the "game scene" feel with a minimal header.
Pros: Title stays present but unobtrusive, drone gets maximum vertical space, feels like you're already in the game world.
Cons: Title may feel too small/unimportant, header strip could read as an afterthought.
3 / "Bottom Controls"
Layout: Title large and centred at ~120px from top with slogan. All controls (mode + drone pickers) pushed into a semi-transparent dark bar at the bottom of the frame (above ground). The middle of the screen is open sky for the drone hero shot. Most cinematic.
Pros: Maximum atmosphere, game scene is the hero, controls are compact and feel like a HUD overlay.
Cons: Controls may feel cramped inside the bar, less discoverable for first-time players.
4 / "Card Layout"
Layout: Title at top. Mode and drone pickers each live inside subtle bordered card containers (1px border, rgba background, rounded corners). Cards stacked vertically in centre. Creates visual grouping and a modern app-like feel.
Pros: Clear visual grouping, modern/polished aesthetic, each section feels self-contained and tappable.
Cons: Cards add visual weight, may feel more "app settings" than "game menu", borders compete with the night scene aesthetic.
